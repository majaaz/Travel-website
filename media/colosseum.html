Historic Landmark 41.8902° N, 12.4922° E

Colosseum

An architectural marvel of the Flavian dynasty, standing as the eternal symbol of Rome's grandeur and the epic theater of gladiatorial combat.

The Legacy

Completed in 80 AD, the Flavian Amphitheatre remains the largest ancient amphitheatre ever built. It was a masterpiece of Roman engineering, capable of holding between 50,000 and 80,000 spectators who gathered to witness public spectacles, re-enactments of famous battles, and the legendary games.

Today, it stands as a testament to the sophistication of Roman urban planning, featuring complex systems of vaulted ceilings and the subterranean "Hypogeum"—a labyrinth of tunnels used to house animals and gladiators before they were hoisted into the arena.
